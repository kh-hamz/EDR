# T1070 - Indicator Removal

Tactic: Defense Evasion

Adversaries delete or truncate logs (`auth.log`, shell history, application
logs) to remove evidence of what they did. On Linux this is frequently as
blunt as `> /var/log/auth.log` or deleting `.bash_history`.

Why it matters: log clearing is a strong secondary signal - legitimate
processes essentially never truncate security-relevant logs, so a hit here
is high-confidence even in isolation, and it usually means something
upstream (that already happened) is worth hunting for.

How this EDR detects it: the `log_clearing` Sigma rule matches file_event
deletes or truncations of well-known log paths and shell history files.
Atomic Red Team validation: T1070.002 (Clear Linux or Mac System Logs).
