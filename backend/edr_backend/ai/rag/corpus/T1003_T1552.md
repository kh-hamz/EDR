# T1003 - OS Credential Dumping / T1552 - Unsecured Credentials

Tactic: Credential Access

Adversaries read files that hold or grant access to credentials:
`/etc/shadow` (password hashes), SSH private keys (`~/.ssh/id_rsa`), or
plaintext secrets left in config files, history files, or environment
files. On Linux this is almost always a file *read*, not a write, which is
part of why it's a quieter step attackers assume goes unnoticed.

Why it matters: successful credential access lets an attacker move
laterally or persist under a legitimate account rather than staying
anomalous. It's frequently the pivot point that turns a single-host
incident into a wider compromise.

How this EDR detects it: the `credential_file_access` Sigma rule matches
file_event reads of sensitive paths (`/etc/shadow`, `~/.ssh/id_rsa`, etc).
Atomic Red Team validation: T1003.008 (`/etc/passwd` and `/etc/shadow`).
