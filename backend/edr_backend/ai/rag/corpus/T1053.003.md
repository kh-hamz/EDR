# T1053.003 - Scheduled Task/Job: Cron

Tactic: Persistence, Privilege Escalation

Adversaries add a crontab entry (user or system) that re-executes their
payload on a schedule, so they keep access even if their original foothold
process is killed. A cron persistence entry is often disguised as a
plausible-looking maintenance job.

Why it matters: persistence mechanisms are what turn a one-time compromise
into a standing foothold. Finding and removing them is required to actually
close out an incident - killing the running process alone isn't enough if
cron will just relaunch it.

How this EDR detects it: the `cron_persistence` Sigma rule watches osquery's
crontab snapshot query for new or suspicious entries (unusual binary paths,
base64/encoded commands, download-and-execute patterns). Atomic Red Team
validation: T1053.003 atomics.
